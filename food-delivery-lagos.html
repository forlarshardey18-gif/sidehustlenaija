How to Start a Food Delivery Side Hustle in Lagos (₦100k–₦500k/month)

Glovo, Jumia Food, Chowdeck, Bolt Food — learn how to start as a rider or small fleet owner in Lagos 2026 and scale fast.

Why Food Delivery is Still Hot in Lagos 2026

People want food delivered fast — offices, hostels, estates. Riders earn ₦100k–₦300k/month, small fleet owners ₦300k–₦1M+. Low startup (₦50k–₦200k for bike + phone).

Two Main Ways to Start

  1. Become a Rider (Fastest Start)
    Sign up on Glovo, Jumia Food, Chowdeck, Bolt Food. Requirements: Bike, smartphone, valid ID. Earn ₦500–₦2,000 per delivery + tips. Work 6–10 hours/day → ₦100k–₦300k/month.
  2. Start a Small Fleet (Scale Up)
    Buy 2–5 bikes (₦150k–₦300k each used), hire riders, take 20–30% commission per delivery. Partner with restaurants directly or use apps. Scale to ₦300k–₦1M/month with 10+ riders.

Step-by-Step to Launch in 1 Month

  1. Week 1: Choose Platform & Register
    Download apps, submit documents (bike papers, ID, passport). Get approved in 1–3 days.
  2. Week 2: Get Equipment
    Bike (used ok), helmet, delivery bag (₦5k–₦10k), power bank, good phone. Total startup: ₦50k–₦200k.
  3. Week 3: Start Delivering
    Work peak hours (lunch 12–3pm, dinner 6–10pm). Focus on high-demand areas (Ikeja, Lekki, Yaba, Surulere).
  4. Week 4: Scale & Optimize
    Track earnings, get reviews, add more riders if fleet model. Save for second bike.

Pro Tips for Lagos Riders

  • Work in high-demand zones: Lekki Phase 1, Victoria Island, Ikeja GRA, Yaba.
  • Use two phones (one for app, one for calls) + power bank.
  • Save 30% of earnings for bike maintenance/fuel.
  • Build good relationship with restaurants for priority orders.
