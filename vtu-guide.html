How to Start a VTU Business in Lagos & Earn ₦300k/month in 2026

VTU (Virtual Top-Up) is one of the easiest side hustles in Nigeria right now. Sell airtime, data, bills from your phone — no shop needed. Here's the full guide to launch and scale in Lagos.

What is VTU Business?

VTU lets you sell airtime, data, electricity, cable TV, and more digitally. Platforms like VTpass or Recharge2Cash give you wholesale rates — you sell at retail and pocket the difference (2-5% per transaction).

Why it's hot in 2026: Everyone in Lagos needs data/airtime daily. Low entry (start with ₦5k), passive once you build customers via WhatsApp/Telegram groups.

Step-by-Step to Start

  1. Choose a Platform: Sign up on VTpass.com or Recharge2Cash. They handle everything — you just fund your wallet.
  2. Fund Your Wallet: Start small (₦5k-₦20k) via bank transfer. Get discounts like 3% on MTN airtime.
  3. Market It: Create a WhatsApp group for your estate/church/school. Post "Buy airtime/data cheap! DM me." Use Instagram stories for Lagos peeps.
  4. Process Sales: Customer pays you via Opay/Moniepoint → you top up instantly via app → done in 30 secs.
  5. Scale Up: Aim for 50+ daily transactions. Hire agents or automate with a Telegram bot.

Pro Tips for Lagos Hustlers

  • Target high-demand areas: Offices, campuses, markets in Ikeja/Yaba.
  • Avoid scams: Use verified platforms only.
  • Legal stuff: Register as a sole proprietor if scaling (CAC is easy online).
  • Tools: Get a second phone line + free Canva for promo flyers.
