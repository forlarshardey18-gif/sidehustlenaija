How to Start a Mini Importation Business from China to Nigeria in 2026 (₦200k–₦1M profit)

Low-risk way to import hot-selling items from China and sell locally/online. Real Naija guide for beginners with small capital.

Why Mini Importation Still Works Big in 2026

China prices are cheap, shipping improved (cheaper sea/air options), and Nigerians love foreign gadgets, fashion, home items. Start with ₦200k–₦500k and flip items for 50–200% profit per sale. Many people quietly make ₦200k–₦1M+ monthly profit.

Best Products to Import Right Now (High Demand, Good Margin)

  • Phone accessories (cases, chargers, earpods) – margin 100–300%
  • Beauty tools (hair wigs, lashes, makeup brushes)
  • Home & kitchen gadgets (blenders, air fryers, LED lights)
  • Fashion (sneakers, bags, jewelry)
  • Baby products (toys, clothes, feeding items)

Step-by-Step: Start Your First Import This Month

  1. Choose Product & Research
    Use AliExpress, 1688.com, Alibaba. Check demand on Jiji, Jumia, Instagram. Target ₦5k–₦20k cost items that sell for ₦15k–₦50k locally.
  2. Find Reliable Supplier
    Order sample first (₦10k–₦30k). Check reviews, chat with supplier. Use agent if new (₦5k–₦15k fee).
  3. Calculate Cost & Profit
    Product + shipping + customs + profit margin. Example: ₦8k cost → ₦25k selling price = ₦17k profit per item.
  4. Import & Clear Customs
    Use DHL/FedEx for small orders (₦20k–₦50k shipping). Or sea freight for bulk. Pay duties (5–20%).
  5. Sell & Scale
    Sell on Instagram, WhatsApp, Jiji, Jumia, Facebook Marketplace. Reinvest profit → order more.

Pro Tips & Risk Management

  • Start small: first order ₦200k–₦300k max to test market.
  • Use agent (e.g. SuperGrok, CargoNaija) if scared of customs.
  • Focus on Instagram + WhatsApp status for fast sales.
  • Avoid banned items (electronics with batteries sometimes tricky).
  • Build trust: good photos, fast delivery, customer reviews.
